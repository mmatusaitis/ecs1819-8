Jackie Robinson

Influencers

Rachel Robinson

Rachel Robsinson and Jackie Robinson went to the same college and got married. Jackie and Rachel faced lots of racism when Jackie went to the majors for baseball. After he retired, Rachel was influenced to create the Jackie Robinson Foundation.

Mallie Robinson

Mallie Robinson is Jackie's mother. She was a mom of five, so as a single mother she struggled greatly. She had to send her five kids away to their uncle where they lived in a very small house. So, Mallie eventually bought a new house in a white neighborhood, and the neighbors were very angry. The fact that Mallie perservered through this inspired Jackie to do the same and join the majors on a white team.

Reverend Karl Downs

Reverend Karl Downs was Jackie's pastor. He noticed that Jackie was getting in a lot of trouble, so he started teaching him about religion and ethnicity. Jackie then was able to get through high school and college smoothly.

Jackie Robinson was the first African American to join the major leagues in baseball since segregation started in 1889. A lot led to that decision. His family was the only black family on thei block, where they received lots of discrimination, but that didn't make them any weaker. He was then the first athlete ever to receive varsity letters in four sports at UCLA, one being baseball. He then had to drop out of college because he could not pay for the expenses, so he joined the army. After 2 years, he had to discharge because of racial discrimination. A little bit later, he was asked to be on the Brooklyn Dodgers, which is what he is most known for.
